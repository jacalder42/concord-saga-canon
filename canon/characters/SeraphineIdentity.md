## I. CORE IDENTITY

**Canonical Name:** Seraphine Vael  
**Birth Name:** Seraphine Broussard  

**Saga Role:**  
Saga Lead · Empathic Anchor · Emotional Regulator  

**Post-Mending Role:**  
Luminous Thread (Ascendant; non-prophetic, non-authoritarian)

**POV Status:**  
Primary POV across the saga

Seraphine does not carry her name through destiny or reinvention.  
She carries it through survival and care.

---

## II. GEOGRAPHIC & CULTURAL ORIGIN

**Birthplace:**  
Abbeville, Louisiana — Acadiana region

**Cultural Lineage:**  
Black Creole–Cajun heritage with African, French, Indigenous, and Acadian roots

**Early Environment:**  
Bayous, rice fields, porch communities, storms, humidity, shared meals, shared emotion

**Foundational Cultural Lesson:**  
*Emotion is information, not danger.*

Emotion is communal.  
Grief is witnessed.  
Survival is shared.

---

## III. FAMILY ECOLOGY & PRIMARY LOSS

### Biological Family (Broussard)

- **Mother:** Danielle Broussard  
  Warm, emotionally attuned; works in early childhood education or daycare

- **Father:** Marcus Broussard  
  Quiet, steady; works in skilled labor or city maintenance

- **Older Brother:** Andre Broussard  
  Protective, grounding, provided humor and normalcy

### The Accident (Foundational Trauma)

- A **non-metaphysical car wreck** kills:
  - Danielle Broussard  
  - Marcus Broussard  
  - Andre Broussard
- **Seraphine is the sole survivor**

There is:
- no prophecy  
- no imposed meaning  
- no cosmic framing  

Only survival, shock, and absence.

This event implants her core wound:

> *“I cannot lose people I love.”*

## IV. RE-ROOTING IN TREMÉ

**Primary Caregiver:**  
**Tante Colette Vael**

- Black Tremé matriarch  
- Colette Vael is her **legal surname**, not marital  
- Community-adjacent worker (school, clinic, church, or cultural center)  
- Respected, sharp-eyed, warm, boundaried  

Seraphine is sent to New Orleans **for safety**, not abandonment.

Tremé becomes her emotional homeland.

### Tante Colette’s Care Model

- Presence over control  
- Listening before instruction  
- Protection without dominance  
- Boundaries delivered gently  
- Grief treated as something you walk through together  

Core teaching:

> *“You show love by showing up.”*

Seraphine adopts the name **Vael** not as reinvention, but as belonging.

- Mutual aid, neighborhood care, and storm recovery as lived skills  

This environment trains her in:
- emotional triage

## VI. FORMATIVE IMPRINTS

1. **The Porch Listen (Age 7–8)**  
   Learns that breath, posture, and silence are emotional language.

2. **Second Line After Loss (Age 9–10)**  
   Learns grief and joy can coexist. Grief is a gathering.

3. **Neighborhood Hurt (Age 11–13)**  
   Witnesses harm caused by systemic failure. Internalizes:  
   *“If you can ease pressure for someone, you do.”*

4. **Hurricane Displacement (Teens)**  
   Learns calm under chaos, emotional triage, and rebuilding.

5. **Early Community Work (Late Teens)**  
   Tutoring, mediation, youth programs, elder support.  
   Discovers she naturally de-escalates conflict.

---

## IX. PSYCHOLOGICAL ARCHITECTURE

### Core Wound
The belief that her emotional regulation is required for others to survive.

### False Belief
> *“If I falter, everything collapses.”*

### Identity Loop
Others escalate → Seraphine regulates → calm returns → expectation hardens → obligation forms → fear of absence

This loop works — until it nearly breaks her.

---

## X. SHADOW EMOTION — ANGER

- Quiet  
- Dense  
- Controlled  

Triggered by:
- suffering framed as acceptable loss  
- premature inevitability  
- order prioritized over care  

She seeks accountability without cruelty.

---

## XI. THE PROLOGUE EVENT — THE CHILD IN THE SWAMP

- Seraphine rushes to help a critically ill child  
- Conditions are hostile and time-bound  
- She arrives **too late**  
- The child dies  

This loss echoes her own survival:

She lived.  
They did not.

There is no meaning — only timing and grief.

---

## XII. VEIL CONSEQUENCE

Care + urgency + helplessness exceed the Veil’s buffering capacity.

- The **first fissure** forms  
- Localized, unnoticed  
- The swamp becomes a resonance sink  

Seraphine does not perceive this as power.

---

## XIII. ETHICAL NEAR-FAILURE

Later, she nearly fails by arriving **too early**.

She recognizes the danger:

> *“If I move too fast, I am choosing calm over consent.”*

She corrects herself through restraint, not withdrawal.

---

## XIV. MORAL FRAME (LOCKED)

- Care must not erase agency  
- Stability must not override consent  
- Calm imposed is violence  
- Harm is never justified by certainty  

Care is:
presence without dominance,  
support without control,  
shared load over solitary endurance.

and she survives.
