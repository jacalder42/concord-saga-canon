## GEOGRAPHIC & CULTURAL ORIGIN

### Birthplace
- Japan, high-density urban environment

### Cultural Context
- Strong emphasis on emotional regulation
- Social preference for quiet endurance over expression
- Harmony prized as moral virtue
- Disruption framed as selfishness

Saeko grows up in a culture where calm is not only valued —  
it is **required**.

---

## FAMILY & EARLY EMOTIONAL ECOLOGY

### Household Environment
- Emotionally volatile domestic space
- Unpredictable interpersonal dynamics
- Raised voices, unresolved conflict
- No stable mediator or emotional anchor

The home is not violent, but it is **loud**.

Saeko learns early:
> *Noise hurts.*

---

### Parental Response
- Care focused on restoring surface calm
- Emotional depth treated as secondary
- Conflict ended through suppression, not resolution

This teaches Saeko that:
- silence ends pain
- expression prolongs it
- calm is relief

## SENSORY OVERLOAD & HYPER-REGULATION

From childhood, Saeko experiences:
- heightened sensitivity to sound
- discomfort with emotional unpredictability
- distress in crowded, expressive environments

Rather than being taught regulation skills, she is taught **avoidance**.

Calm becomes safety.
